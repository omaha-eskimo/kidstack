# School Meeting Ground Truth

## Summary

The meeting should begin from shared observations rather than competing explanations.

## What We Know

- Maya produces detailed, sustained work on open-ended projects.
- Participation drops during some repetitive independent work.
- Noisy transitions have been identified as difficult at both school and home.
- Maya has described lunchtime as containing too many simultaneous conversations.
- Visual previews and quiet decompression are proposed experiments, not proven solutions.

## What We Do Not Yet Know

- Whether insufficient challenge, transition load, social demands, or another factor best explains each event
- How frequently the lunch difficulty occurs
- Whether the same supports work consistently across settings

## Meeting Purpose

Agree on a small number of observable experiments, assign ownership, and choose a review date.
