# Strengths and Needs

## Summary

Maya's strongest engagement appears when she can combine visual thinking, research, and creative construction. Current needs center on transition support, appropriately challenging work, and low-pressure ways to communicate when overloaded.

## Strengths and Drivers

- Strong visual-spatial reasoning
- Sustained attention on self-directed projects
- Detailed recall for topics she finds meaningful
- Empathy toward animals and younger children
- Ability to connect ideas across subjects

## Needs and Friction Points

- Predictability before schedule changes
- Reduced repetition after mastery is demonstrated
- Recovery time after noisy group activities
- Alternatives to immediate verbal explanation when upset
- Adult check-ins that do not draw public attention

## Working Hypotheses

> [!CAUTION]
> These are questions to test, not diagnoses or established facts.

- Some apparent disengagement may reflect insufficient challenge.
- Some after-school irritability may reflect accumulated transition load.
- Visual planning may reduce the effort required to begin multi-step tasks.
